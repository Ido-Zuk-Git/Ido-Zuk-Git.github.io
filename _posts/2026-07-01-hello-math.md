A first post to confirm that LaTeX renders.

Inline math works: Euler's identity is $e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$, and a sum like
$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k = \tfrac{n(n+1)}{2}$ sits neatly in the paragraph.

Display math gets its own line:

$$
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2}\,dx = \sqrt{\pi}.
$$

Aligned equations work too:

$$
\begin{aligned}
(a+b)^2 &= a^2 + 2ab + b^2 \\
(a-b)^2 &= a^2 - 2ab + b^2.
\end{aligned}
$$

If you can read the symbols above as typeset mathematics, MathJax is wired up
correctly.
